Adividiardi

In loving memory

October
Evening

1964 — September 15, 1993

A mahogany bay mare with a white diamond and its small falling trail, Evening was strong, singular, and deeply loved. Christina called her companion. William, as a baby, called her “Eegy.”

On your mark

Christina rode her bareback. “On your mark, get set”—a wiggle of the reins—and Evening would rear, gather herself, and fly. Christina could vault over her hindquarters and land on her back as though horse and rider had rehearsed it forever.

Her own mind

Evening was a good jumper and a good horse. She did not care for other horses, and made no apology for it. She knew what she liked: apples, carrots, hay, open ground, and the people she had chosen.

Her mark

Her coat was a deep reddish bay, her mane dark. A diamond-shaped star brightened her forehead, with a short white trail beneath it; one white sock marked her left hind leg.

At rest

Her affection was quiet and unmistakable. She would place her head on a shoulder and leave it there—her great weight made gentle, her trust given without reserve.

For Evening

You carried no need to be tame,
only the kindness of choosing us.

At a whisper and a loosened rein
you gathered the field beneath you—
red as the last light,
quick as a promise kept.

Now evening comes by your old name:
a diamond in the deepening sky,
one bright trail beneath it,
and the remembered weight of your head
resting softly on a shoulder.

For Evening — from Christina and William
