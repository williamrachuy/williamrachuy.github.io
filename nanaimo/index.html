Nanaimo Bars

Canada's most beloved no-bake treat — three perfect layers
⏱ 30 min active
❄️ 2 hr chill
🍫 No baking required
🍁 Classic Canadian

⚖️ Serving Calculator

How many bars?
1 pan · 8×8" · standard recipe

Ingredients

Layer 1 Chocolate Graham Base
    Layer 2 Vanilla Custard Cream
      Layer 3 Dark Chocolate Ganache

        Instructions

        Layer 1 — Chocolate Base
        1
        🥣Set up a double boiler

        Fill a medium saucepan with 1–2 inches of water and bring to a gentle simmer. Place a heatproof bowl on top — it should sit above, not touch, the water.

        2
        🧈Melt butter with sugar and cocoa

        Add butter, sugar, and cocoa to the bowl. Stir frequently until the butter is fully melted and the mixture is smooth and glossy. Remove from heat.

        3
        🥚Temper in the egg

        Let the chocolate mixture cool for 2 minutes. Add a pinch of salt, then whisk in the beaten egg vigorously. Return the bowl to the double boiler briefly, stirring, until the mixture thickens slightly — about 1 minute. It should coat the back of a spoon.

        4
        🍪Mix in dry ingredients

        Remove from heat. Stir in the graham cracker crumbs, shredded coconut, and chopped nuts until fully combined — the mixture will be thick and fudgy.

        5
        📐Press into pan

        Line an 8×8" baking pan with parchment paper, leaving an overhang on two sides. Press the base mixture firmly and evenly into the bottom — a flat-bottomed glass works great for smoothing it out.

        6
        ❄️Refrigerate

        Chill the base in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes until firm before adding the next layer.

        Layer 2 — Vanilla Custard Cream
        7
        🧁Beat the custard buttercream

        Using a hand mixer or stand mixer, beat softened butter until pale and fluffy, about 2 minutes. Add the custard powder (or instant vanilla pudding mix) and heavy cream, and beat to combine.

        8
        🌟Add icing sugar

        Gradually add powdered sugar, beating on low at first to avoid a cloud of sugar, then increasing to medium-high until the filling is light, thick, and spreadable. If it's too stiff, add ½ tsp more cream.

        9
        🖌️Spread over base

        Remove the chilled base from the fridge. Spread the custard layer evenly across the top using an offset spatula. Smooth it as flat as possible — this makes the ganache look cleaner.

        10
        ❄️Chill again

        Return to the refrigerator for 30 minutes until the custard layer is firm to the touch.

        Layer 3 — Dark Chocolate Ganache
        11
        🍫Melt chocolate and butter

        Combine chopped chocolate and butter in a heatproof bowl. Melt over a double boiler (or microwave in 20-second bursts, stirring between each), until perfectly smooth. Let cool for 3–5 minutes — you want it pourable but not steaming hot.

        12
        🌊Pour and spread

        Pour the ganache over the custard layer. Tilt the pan gently or use a spatula to spread it into a thin, even layer right to the edges. Work quickly — it will start to set.

        Finishing
        13
        ⏳The final chill

        Refrigerate the whole pan for at least 1 hour until the ganache is completely set. For the cleanest slices, chill overnight.

        14
        🔪Slice — this is the moment

        Lift the slab out using the parchment overhang. Place on a cutting board. Let sit at room temperature for 5 minutes so the ganache softens just enough to cut cleanly without cracking. Run a sharp knife under hot water, dry it, then slice into bars with one firm downward motion per cut — don't saw. Repeat between cuts.

        15
        🍽️Serve and enjoy

        Serve slightly chilled or at room temperature. The custard layer is what makes these iconic — it should be creamy and almost melting against the firm ganache above and the dense base below.

        Tips & Notes

        🏆 Pro Tips

        • Custard powder is traditional — Bird's brand is the Canadian classic. Vanilla instant pudding works as a substitute but the flavour is slightly different.
        • Graham cracker crumbs — you can pulse whole crackers in a food processor, or buy pre-made crumbs. In Canada, digestive biscuits work perfectly too.
        • Slice cold, serve cool. These are best eaten within 5 minutes of coming out of the fridge — the custard layer softens fast at room temperature.
        • The warm knife trick is essential. A cold or dry knife will crack the ganache. Dip in hot water, dry immediately, cut.
        • Nut-free? Omit the walnuts and add an extra ¼ cup of graham crumbs or coconut to the base layer.
        • Coconut-free? Omit the coconut — the base will still hold together fine with slightly more graham crumbs.

        📦 Storage

        • Store in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 5 days.
        • Layer bars between sheets of parchment paper so they don't stick together.
        • These freeze beautifully for up to 3 months. Wrap individually in plastic wrap, then store in a freezer bag.
        • Thaw in the refrigerator overnight — do not microwave.
