How to Play

The Table

Four houses — Spades (you), Hearts, Clubs and Diamonds — sit around a flattened pyramid. Each house's suit is one triangular face: left and right are its base corners, center is its apex. Adjacent faces share a base corner; all four apexes meet at the peak.

Your Turn

Turn order is Spades → Hearts → Clubs → Diamonds. Each turn you get 2 actions:

  • Pivot (1 action) — swap two adjacent cards at a legal node touching one of your own cards.
  • Draw (1 action) — take a card from the deck into your hand (max 3).
  • Buy Action (1 gem) — gain an extra action this turn, up to twice.
  • Claim (free) — take an armed gem for score, any time it's available.
  • Pass (free) — end your turn early.

Gems

Gems arm automatically when the cards around a node match — Aces are wild. A spent gem must go unmatched before it can arm again.

Royals & Trump

Your Champion is the royal card in your center royal slot. Rock-paper-scissors: King beats Queen, Queen beats Jack, Jack beats King. Otherwise, suits trump in the cycle Spades → Hearts → Clubs → Diamonds → Spades, unless the royal RPS overrides it.

  • Claiming a rival's court gem costs 1 action — unless your Champion trumps theirs, then it's free.
  • When a peak gem is claimed, any house whose Champion trumps the claimant's steals it (first in turn order wins). A dramatic duel.

The Pyramid

When all 4 base corners agree and all 4 centers agree (all cap + both peak nodes matched), the pyramid becomes coherent. The house whose action completes it scores +150 and the game ends immediately.

Otherwise

When the deck runs out, every house gets one final turn, then the highest score wins. Ties break on gem count, then Champion trump.
